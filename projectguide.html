Step One- Software and Adjustment

The product was designed using Autodesk Fusion. It is currently designed for the iPhone SE, so for any other iPhone the parameters must be adjusted. The current parameters are: length (the long horizontal distance of the box), width (the short horizontal distance of the box), height (the vertical distance of the box), Thickness (the depth of each side, the lid and the bottom of the box)

Step Two- 3D Printing

After making adjustments, save the file and open the .stl in PrusaSlicer. Use the suggested supports, as they can be easily removed from the model.

Step Three - Solder the Timers Circuit board

Using your Soldering Iron, follow the instructions below to solder the circuit board to the timer to follow its intended use. (What would follow is the instructions to solder the circuit board of the timer correctly and wire it to the lock)

Step Four - Attach the Timer and Lock to the Lock Box

Use the reference model to screw the timer and lock into their correct positions on the lockbox.

Step Five - Wire the Timer and Lock together

Follow the below instructions to connect the timer and lock, and test the function of the lock when connected to the Lock Box. (What would follow is the instructions of wiring the lock and the timer together)

Step Six - Complete the Hinge of the Box

Place the rod through the printed hinges on the lock box, and use the hot glue gun to seal both sides of the hinges and prevent the rod from falling out.

Step Seven - Enjoy the Product!
